Nimble Hybrid Architecture: The Future of Data Storage

Introduction

Nimble Hybrid Architecture is a new approach to data storage that combines the best of both worlds: the performance and scalability of flash storage with the cost-effectiveness of traditional disk storage.

Benefits of Nimble Hybrid Architecture

Increased performance: Nimble Hybrid Architecture can provide up to 10x the performance of traditional disk storage. This is because Nimble uses a variety of technologies to optimize performance, such as caching, de-duplication, and compression.

Scalability: Nimble Hybrid Architecture can scale to meet the needs of even the most demanding workloads. Nimble systems can scale up to 100PB of capacity and 100,000 IOPS.

Cost-effectiveness: Nimble Hybrid Architecture is more cost-effective than traditional disk storage because it uses a combination of flash and disk storage. Flash storage is more expensive than disk storage, but it is also much faster. Nimble uses a variety of technologies to optimize flash storage utilization, which helps to reduce costs.

Simplicity: Nimble Hybrid Architecture is easy to manage and maintain. Nimble systems come with a variety of features that make it easy to set up, manage, and monitor your storage environment.

Nimble Hybrid Architecture is a good choice for organizations that need the performance and scalability of flash storage without the high cost. Nimble Hybrid Architecture is also a good choice for organizations that need to consolidate their storage environment into a single platform.

How To

Here are some examples of how Nimble Hybrid Architecture can be used to improve the performance, scalability, and cost-effectiveness of your data storage environment:

Virtualization: Nimble Hybrid Architecture can be used to improve the performance and scalability of your virtualized environment. Nimble can provide up to 10x the performance of traditional disk storage, which can help to improve the performance of your virtual machines. Nimble can also scale to meet the needs of even the most demanding virtualized environments.

Databases: Nimble Hybrid Architecture can be used to improve the performance and scalability of your database environment. Nimble can provide up to 10x the performance of traditional disk storage, which can help to improve the performance of your database applications. Nimble can also scale to meet the needs of even the most demanding database environments.

Cloud storage: Nimble Hybrid Architecture can be used to provide a hybrid cloud storage solution. Nimble systems can be used to store your most critical data on-premises, while using cloud storage to store your less critical data. This can help to reduce the cost of your storage environment without sacrificing performance or reliability.

If you are looking for a new way to improve the performance, scalability, and cost-effectiveness of your data storage environment, then Nimble Hybrid Architecture is a good option to consider.

Examples

on-premises HPE Nimble Storage arrays offer superior performance and latency for critical workloads. Cloud compute resources can access the on-premises data without the need to migrate that data to the cloud. HPE InfoSight offers visibility into the data and predictive analytics 1. Additionally, leveraging a multi-cloud flash fabric with HPE Nimble Storage and HPE Cloud Volumes enables seamless, bi-directional data mobility between on-premises and cloud resources. This unlocks hybrid cloud use cases from test/dev and production to analytics and data protection 2.

Is it the right choice for you?

Nimble Hybrid architecture may not be for everyone. When choosing it consider below potential drawbacks:

  • increased complexity in managing both on-premises and cloud-based components
  • potentially higher operational costs due to the need for expertise in managing both environments
  • and the need for robust security measures to ensure data protection across different platforms
  • Additionally, seamless integration between the two environments can sometimes pose challenges and may require additional resources and effort

Conclusion

The cloud is not the answer to everything (at least not yet), but it is clear that the new reality of IT revolves around leveraging on-premises and cloud architectures together in a hybrid model. Hybrid cloud architectures that span the public cloud, the private cloud (on-premises or hosted), and on-premises resources deliver the most realistic and robust technology capabilities for businesses today.

There is no doubt that you can gain significant benefits from deploying on-premises or colocated Nimble Storage arrays in support of cloud compute resources. Such an architecture enables you to take advantage of the enterprise features, availability, accessibility, and scalability of best-in-class Nimble Storage solutions to retain control over one of the most—if not the most—critical asset that your company has: your data.
